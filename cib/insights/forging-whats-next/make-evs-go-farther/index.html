Wolfspeed | Racing to make EVs go farther

CEO Gregg Lowe

Transcript: Wolfspeed: Racing to make EVs go farther

Gregg Lowe

CEO
Wolfspeed

The internal combustion engine is going away. Some people call that the ICE. And our company motto is: We're helping end the ICE age.

Announcer

Welcome race fans to Formula E – the all-electric future of racing.

Back to Gregg

Formula E is an innovation lab on wheels. We find out all sorts of things when you take a product to the extremes.

Wolfspeed is a semiconductor company and we specialize in a technology called silicon carbide. It's a better technology than silicon because it's more efficient. It wastes less energy. For instance, if you use silicon carbide in an electric car instead of silicon, your car is gonna travel 5 to 15% further.

We're a sponsor of the Jaguar TCS Formula E racing team. We have two embedded engineers on the race team. We’re only one year in but we’re learning a lot.

Preeti Raghupathi

Managing Director
Wells Fargo Corporate & Investment Banking

When Gregg joined Wolfspeed six years ago, it was a very different company. It was called Cree and it was best known for making LED light bulbs.

He made a bet – a huge bet -- both on the technology but also on the market taking off for that technology.

Back to Gregg

Our new factory in upstate New York is the world's only 200-millimeter silicon carbide factory. That factory has essentially sold out.

We’ve announced supply agreements with General Motors, Jaguar Land Rover, Lucid and with others. Our intention is to grow our silicon carbide chip business, from now through 2027, at a 60% compounded annual growth rate.

Back to Preeti

Wells Fargo has raised about $5 billion of capital for Wolfspeed through about half a dozen transactions since 2018.

Back to Gregg

Raising capital as a relatively small company is not for the faint of heart. Wells Fargo is kind of like the calm in the middle of the storm.

At Wolfspeed, it’s really simple: We power more by using less.
