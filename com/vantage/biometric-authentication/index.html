What is Sign On with Touch ID?

How do I enable the Sign On with Touch ID feature?

After registering your fingerprint on your iOS device, open the app and follow the instructions to add Touch ID. You can also set up Touch ID from your User Profile.

Note: Any and all fingerprints registered on your device can be used to sign on to the app. We recommend not using Touch ID if you share your device.

How do I register a fingerprint on my device?

Select Touch ID & Passcode in your Apple deviceâ€™s settings. Then follow the steps to register one or more fingerprints.

How do I sign on once I've set up Touch ID?

Open the app. Tap Touch ID, then hold your finger on the sensor button to sign on.

Can I use different fingerprints to sign on?

Yes. You can sign on using any fingerprint registered on your device.

Should I use the Sign On with Touch ID feature if I share my device?

No. When Touch ID is enabled, anyone with a fingerprint registered on your device can sign on to the app, view your accounts, and use your services. We do not recommend using Touch ID if you share your device.

What should I do if I lose my device, or my device is stolen?

Touch ID can be disabled remotely with the Find My app. If you have the Find My app installed on your device, you can use it to remotely lock your device. Please refer to the Apple website for more information about Find My iPhone.

No. Wells Fargo does not have access to the information needed to recognize your fingerprint.

Can I still use my password to sign on?

Yes. You can sign on with your password at any time. When you open the app, enter your password to sign on with your Vantage credentials.

Can I remove the Touch ID feature and use only my password?

Yes. You can remove the Touch ID feature from your User Profile. After signing on to the app, navigate to your User Profile, select App settings, and toggle Touch ID off.

If a change to your registered fingerprints is detected, you will have to sign on with your Vantage credentials, then re-enable Touch ID in the app.

Touch ID will be disabled automatically after multiple failed attempts. Use your device passcode to unlock Touch ID, then re-open the app to sign on with Touch ID.
