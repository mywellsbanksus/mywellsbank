What is the Wells Fargo Cost of Savings Index?

Wells Fargo determines certain adjustable mortgage rates using the Wells Fargo Cost of Savings Index (Wells COSI). The interest rate on your loan is the sum of the index value plus an additional amount called a margin.

The Wells COSI is based on the interest rates the depository subsidiaries of Wells Fargo & Company pay to individuals on certificates of deposit (CDs), also known as personal time deposits. The index is calculated using the weighted average of all the interest rates paid on CDs held by individual depositors as of the last business day of each month. The index is calculated monthly and is used to determine the interest rate on your mortgage.
